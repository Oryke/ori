# ORI Principles

Every project eventually reaches moments where the right path isn't obvious.

A new feature sounds useful but adds complexity.
A shortcut delivers results quickly but sacrifices clarity.
A metric looks impressive but hides important context.

These moments are where principles matter.

ORI is guided by a small set of enduring beliefs that influence every design decision, feature, and contribution. They are intended to remain stable even as the implementation evolves.

---

## 1. Context Before Conclusions

A repository is more than its code.

Every insight ORI produces should be grounded in the surrounding context; documentation, discussions, decisions, history, and governance — not isolated observations.

Understanding should always come before evaluation.

---

## 2. Explain Every Insight

An unexplained recommendation has limited value.

Whenever ORI identifies an opportunity or raises a concern, it should be able to explain how it arrived at that conclusion.

Maintainers should never be expected to trust a result they cannot understand.

---

## 3. Preserve Human Judgment

ORI exists to assist maintainers, not replace them.

Repository Intelligence should augment human decision-making by surfacing information that may otherwise remain hidden. Final decisions should always belong to the people who understand the project's goals and context.

---

## 4. Respect Every Repository

No two repositories are identical.

Projects differ in their purpose, community, governance, and stage of development. ORI should avoid assuming that one workflow, documentation style, or contribution model fits every project.

Repository Intelligence should adapt to context rather than enforcing uniformity.

---

## 5. Preserve Knowledge

Knowledge lost is opportunity lost.

Whenever possible, ORI should encourage the preservation of architectural reasoning, historical decisions, contributor knowledge, and project memory so that future maintainers inherit understanding rather than uncertainty.

---

## 6. Make Complexity Understandable

Software projects naturally become more complex over time.

ORI should help make that complexity easier to understand without oversimplifying it. The objective is clarity, not reduction.

---

## 7. Remain Transparent

Repository Intelligence should never feel mysterious.

Wherever practical, ORI should reveal the evidence behind its observations, making it possible for contributors to verify, challenge, and improve the reasoning process.

Transparency builds trust.

---

## 8. Evolve Through Community

ORI is an open-source project.

Its ideas should grow through discussion, experimentation, and contributions from maintainers, researchers, and developers around the world.

Repository Intelligence is stronger when it reflects collective learning rather than individual opinion.

---

# Looking Ahead

These principles are intentionally technology-independent.

Programming languages will change.
Frameworks will evolve.
AI capabilities will improve.

The principles that guide ORI should endure.

Every future feature should be evaluated against these principles before it becomes part of the project.
