# Repository Intelligence

## Overview

Every repository tells two stories.

The first is easy to see. It lives in the source code, commit history, releases, and pull requests. Version control systems were designed to preserve this story.

The second story is much harder to see.

It lives in the reasoning behind important decisions, the conversations that shaped the architecture, the lessons maintainers learned over time, the documentation that explains why something exists, and the shared understanding that allows people to contribute with confidence.

Repository Intelligence is the name ORI gives to that second story.

It is a way of understanding a repository not only as a collection of files, but as a living body of knowledge that grows with every contribution.

---

## Why Repository Intelligence?

Most repositories begin with code.

Over time, they accumulate something much more valuable: understanding.

As projects grow, contributors come and go. Maintainers change. Features evolve. Decisions made years ago continue to influence work today, even when the people who made those decisions are no longer involved.

Without context, repositories become harder to understand.

Questions get asked repeatedly.

Past mistakes are unknowingly repeated.

Knowledge becomes concentrated in a few people instead of being shared across the community.

Repository Intelligence exists to help make that knowledge more visible, easier to preserve, and easier to transfer.

---

## The Core Idea

Code explains what a project does.

Repository Intelligence helps explain why it became that way.

Rather than looking only at source code, Repository Intelligence encourages us to look at the repository as a whole:

- Documentation
- Pull requests
- Issues
- Design decisions
- Governance
- Community discussions
- Historical context

Together, these artifacts tell the story of how a project thinks, learns, and evolves.

---

## The Seven Dimensions

ORI describes Repository Intelligence through seven connected dimensions.

1. Documentation Depth — How well knowledge is captured and maintained.
2. Decision Traceability — Whether important decisions can be understood after they are made.
3. Contributor Pathways — How easily new contributors can become productive.
4. Governance Clarity — How clearly decisions, responsibilities, and processes are defined.
5. Historical Continuity — How well a project's past remains understandable over time.
6. Community Signal — What a repository's interactions reveal about its health and culture.
7. Knowledge Distribution — How widely important knowledge is shared across contributors.

These dimensions should not be viewed in isolation. Improvements in one often strengthen the others.

---

## Why It Matters

Healthy repositories preserve more than code.

They preserve understanding.

When knowledge is easier to discover, projects become easier to maintain, contributors gain confidence more quickly, and repositories become more resilient as communities grow and change.

Repository Intelligence is intended to provide a shared language for thinking about these challenges, so they can be discussed intentionally instead of being rediscovered by every project independently.

---

## What Repository Intelligence Is Not

Repository Intelligence is not a score.

It is not another productivity metric.

It is not a replacement for documentation, governance, or developer experience.

Instead, it is a conceptual framework that brings these existing ideas together and encourages us to view them as parts of a larger knowledge system.

ORI does not attempt to replace established software engineering practices.

It aims to help connect them.

---

## Looking Ahead

This document introduces the central idea behind Repository Intelligence.

As ORI evolves, this concept will continue to be refined through research, practical experimentation, and feedback from maintainers, contributors, educators, and organizations across the open-source ecosystem.

Repository Intelligence is not presented as a finished answer.

It is an invitation to better understand the knowledge that already exists inside every repository.
